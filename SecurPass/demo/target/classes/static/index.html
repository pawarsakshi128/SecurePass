What are Strong Passwords?

Secure your data!!!

    1. Length:- A strong password is usually at least 12 characters long. Longer passwords are harder for attackers to crack.
    2. Complexity:- A strong password should contain lowercase alphabet, uppercase alphabet , numeric digit and special characters.
    3. Unpredictability:- A strong password should not be easily guessable. Password should unique and random.
    4. Avoidance of patterns:- Strong passwords do not follow predictable patterns, such as “123456” or ”abcd”.

Examples of strong passwords are:

  • $/\/\!Td@t!R2024
  • /-\|\|@nD|^aD@v21^
  • $@|^aS|-|!!pa\/\/R#2003

Why Strong passwords are necessary:-

Primary reasons of strong password is safeguarding of data.

    1. Protection against unauthorized access.
    2. Prevention of data breaches.
    3. Mitigation of identity theft.
    4. Protection across multiple platforms.
    5. Defense against automated attacks.

How to create strong password?

    1. Length: Your password must be at least 12 characters long. 14 or more is better.
    2. Combination: Uses of combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers and special characters.
    3. Don’t use a word that can be found easily in dictionary, the name of a person or organization.
    4. Do not include your name, a family member’s name because these are too easy to guess and crack
    5. Do not use phone numbers and birthdays as your password.
    6. Your password must be updated every three months as suggested by the Cybersecurity expert.
    7. User can also get extra security by implementing two-factor authentication.
